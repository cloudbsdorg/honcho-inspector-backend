--- 4. What this proves ---
- A single @Component class can implement HonchoProvider and serve
  multiple related operations (2+ ops, not 1-per-file).
- The interface is implementable: all 5 abstract/default methods
  have a clear, well-typed contract.
- The dispatch-on-op pattern (switch in execute()) works: test calls
  execute() with each op and asserts the right branch was exercised.
- The "throw on unowned op" contract is enforceable: passing an op the
  provider doesn't own throws HonchoCallException with status 501.
- The default pathTemplate/httpMethod behavior (throw on missing
  override) is testable: a bare HonchoProvider without overrides
  throws UnsupportedOperationException with a helpful message.
